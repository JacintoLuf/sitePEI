T U C

Trotinetes Universitárias Conectadas

The University of Aveiro has two campus in the urban perimeter of the city of Aveiro, designated as the Santiago campus and the Crasto campus. A pedestrian traveling between the furthest points of these two campus normally takes about 24 minutes. Despite the majority of those who use both campus, the average travel time is shorter and therefore not a problem, but, there are cases in which the use of an eletric scooter would reduce the travel time to about 1/4.

To reduce the usage costs, it is desirable to adopt a scooter sharing solution. However, after reaearching the solutions available on the market, it was concluded that it was difficult to find a suitable solution for this scenario.

Architecture

GPS

GPS module is connected to the arduino and allows the arduino to know its location.

Bluetooth

Bluetooth module is connected to the arduino, and allows the pairing of it with the scooter, to send orders to it.

Microcontroller

Microcontroller is the brains of the operation, it interconnects all the modules.

GSM/GPRS

GSM/GPRS module is connected to the arduino, and allows the arduino to be connected to the internet, to send all the important information (location, battery, etc) of the scooter.
